laughing on
line

By Kishan Sheth, Russell Goldenberg, Caitlyn Ralph

Laughter is complex. Even IRL it is hard to tell real ones and fake ones apart. Online, it is even harder with fewer visual cues and a wider range of tools to express laughter. As internet language develops, new forms of laughter spread like a cold in a classroom. If you are stuck on the “ha”s and “lol”s of days past, consider this your guide to the present. This is the perception and evolution of the digital laugh.

Perception

As with most lingo, the intent and perception of each laugh varies from person to person. Help us do some casual science to get a sense of how you use some of the most common laughs compared to other internet people.

Pick a laugh. Not familiar? Skip it!

Tell us how funny something really is when you use

  • Unamused, not smiling; perhaps even feeling spiteful.
  • Neutral. Used as filler or to be nice. No physical response.
  • A single breath of air exits the mouth, a possible smile forms.
  • Visibly amused. Puffs of air released, slight body giration.
  • Laughing so much that breathing is hard; actually rofl-ing.
